Michael Jackson

Michael Jackson, known as the "King of Pop," was an American singer, songwriter, and dancer who became one of the most iconic and influential entertainers in music history.

Born on August 29, 1958, he began his career with the Jackson 5 before launching a groundbreaking solo career. His 1982 album Thriller remains the best-selling album of all time.

Known for his signature moves like the moonwalk, innovative music videos, and a unique voice, Jackson redefined pop culture. Despite controversies and legal battles later in life, his legacy as a musical genius and global icon endures.
